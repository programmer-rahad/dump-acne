It’s not just you

5.6 million Canadians have acne, and at least 50% of them have acne on their bodies.1

“It’s a
deal-breaker.”

Red Flag #1

It won’t give
you space

Acne can show up on your face, back AND chest

45% of people with acne have it on their chests.2

61% of people with acne have it on their backs.2

92% of people with acne have it on their faces.2

“My friends
are talking.”

Red Flag #2

It gets around

Acne is so common, it seems like everybody has a story to tell

> 80% of people with acne develop it between the ages of 12–24.1

1 in 4 people with acne will still have it by the age of 25.1

20-30% of adults between the ages of 20–40 have acne.1

95% of people with acne will develop some degree of scarring.3

“It blew up
out of nowhere!”

Red Flag #3

Easily triggered

Everyday things can set acne off

Some cosmetics can cause acne breakouts and aggravate existing acne.1

Pressure and friction on the skin, such as from sports equipment, can lead to acne development.1

Sweating can aggravate acne, especially if the sweat is trapped under damp and dirty clothes.1

Genetics: Acne can run in the family, and people with a family history of acne have a greater risk of developing it.1

Medications containing certain ingredients can cause acne, aggravate acne, or cause acne-like symptoms.1

Stress: Hormones associated with stress can worsen acne.1

“I didn’t
know what
to believe!”

Red Flag #4

It fills your head with lies

Do you know the truth about acne?

LIE! Greasy foods cause acne There is no scientific evidence that greasy foods cause acne or make it worse.1

LIE! Poor hygiene can cause acne Acne is not caused by dirty skin or reflective of poor personal hygiene.1

LIE! Acne affects only the skin Acne impacts more than just the skin. It can lower self-esteem and affect psychological and social development.1

LIE! Washing frequently and using over- the-counter acne products will clear up acne Washing too often can actually make acne worse. Acne is a chronic disease that involves many factors and may require prescription treatment to clear it.1
